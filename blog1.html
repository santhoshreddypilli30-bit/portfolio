2026 24 Hours of Le Mans

Toyota's road back to the top

For nearly an entire day Toyota fought punctures, sensor problems, safety car swings and relentless pressure from BMW and Cadillac. Victory looked unlikely more than once. Then Le Mans chose its winner.

This was not supposed to be Toyota's race.

BMW started from pole. Cadillac looked unstoppable through long stretches of the night. Toyota's two TR010 Hybrids started deep in the field and spent much of the race chasing rather than controlling.

Then came the setbacks.

Early in the fourth hour, the #7 Toyota suffered a slow puncture. Soon after, a driveshaft sensor issue dropped the car further down the order. The crew that would eventually win Le Mans spent hours simply trying to stay alive in the fight.

"The race was relentless. We had lots of challenges come our way, but somehow we always kept going." Nyck de Vries

The race swung repeatedly. Safety cars compressed the field. Cadillac lost momentum. BMW remained within striking distance. Every strategy call mattered.

By Sunday morning, Toyota's race was balanced on a knife edge. One more setback would have ended the dream.

Instead, Kamui Kobayashi delivered. De Vries attacked. The team found itself back in contention when it seemed impossible.

"There were many moments I thought we were out of contention." Nyck de Vries

With the final hours counting down, four cars fought for one trophy. The #7 Toyota emerged ahead and refused to let go.

When Kobayashi crossed the finish line after 381 laps, Toyota's four-year wait for another Le Mans victory was over.

"Finally we're back on top." Kamui Kobayashi
